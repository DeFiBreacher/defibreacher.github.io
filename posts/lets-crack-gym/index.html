Let's Crack Gym

Posted May 18, 2026
By DeFiBreacher
10 min read
Let's Crack Gym
Let's Crack Gym

Five days a week, no excuses, no skipped sessions. This is my current split.

Before we get into it: the weight you use is completely on you. Don’t feel bad if you lift less than the guy next to you or your friend. Everyone starts somewhere. Everyone has different genetics, different history, different goals. The only person you’re competing with is who you were yesterday. Pick a weight that challenges you, not one that impresses someone else.

On sets and reps: the sets and reps I’ve listed are based on what works for me. Take them as a suggestion, not a rule. One thing I will say though — people do way too many sets. More sets does not mean more muscle. With a good weight and proper form, 3-4 sets per exercise is more than enough. Focus on quality over quantity. Ten sets of half-effort is worse than 3 sets of full effort.

On “Till Failure”: this means you push yourself as hard as you can until you physically cannot do another rep with good form. Don’t stop because it burns or because it’s uncomfortable — stop when you genuinely have nothing left. This is where real muscle growth happens. The last 2-3 reps you didn’t think you had in you are the ones that actually matter.

On muscle fiber stretch: the more slowly and fully you move through each rep, the more you stretch the muscle fiber. If you are not fully stretching the muscle at the bottom of the movement, you are leaving gains on the table. Half reps build half muscles. Go deep, go slow, feel the stretch — your gains will be on a completely different level.

On exercise rankings:

⭐ Superior: This exercise has exceptional muscle contraction and feel. It hits the muscle the way it’s meant to be hit. If you’re short on time, always prioritize these.

🟣 Heavy: I recommend hitting this exercise as heavy as possible with progressive overload. These are your money movements, the ones where adding weight over time makes the biggest difference.

Progressive Overload explained: Progressive overload simply means doing a little more over time. That could be adding 2.5kg to the bar, doing one extra rep, or doing the same weight with better form. Your muscles grow when they’re forced to adapt to a new challenge. If you’re lifting the same weight with the same reps every single week, you’re not growing, you’re just maintaining. Track your lifts. Push a little more each week. That’s the entire secret to building muscle.

Monday — Chest / Front & Side Shoulders

The week starts with a push. Chest and shoulders set the tone.

Exercise Sets Reps  
Flat Bench Press 3 🔴 Till Failure 🟣 Heavy
Incline Dumbbell Press 4 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior
Cable Chest Fly 3 12-15  
Seated Fly 3 12-15  
Shoulder / Military Press 4 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior 🟣 Heavy
Lateral Raises 4 12-15 ⭐ Superior
Front Raises (optional) 3 12  

Note on Front Raises: If you’re short on time, skip this one guilt-free. Your front shoulder gets trained on every single pressing exercise you do: bench press, incline press, military press. By the time you reach front raises, it’s already had a solid workout. Lateral raises and military press are the priority here.

Rest: Rest whenever you feel like you’ve had enough but watch the time. Don’t let rest become a scroll session. Focus on recovering, not disappearing.

On time: I don’t think any session should take more than 1 hour 15 minutes to wrap up. If it is, something is off — could be too many sets, too long on rest, or too much time on your phone between sets. In the beginning it’s completely fine, you might be exhausted after each set and need longer to recover. But as you progress, aim to keep it tight. In and out. Quality over duration.

Tuesday — Back / Rear Shoulders

Pull day. This is where you build the thickness and width that makes everything look bigger.

Exercise Sets Reps  
Deadlift (optional) 4 6-8  
Lat Pulldown 4 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior 🟣 Heavy
Chest Supported Row Machine 3 🔴 Till Failure 🟣 Heavy
Chest Supported / T-Bar Row 3 🔴 Till Failure  
Barbell Shrugs 4 12-15  
Reverse Fly 4 15  
Hyperextension 3 12-15  

Note on Deadlift: Deadlift is optional and I mean that genuinely. You do not need to go heavier and heavier on deadlifts to build a great back. Believe me, I’ve been years into this. The ego lift culture around deadlifts has hurt more people than it has helped. A good lat pulldown, a solid row, and consistent progressive overload on supported movements will build your back just as well — with far less injury risk. If you enjoy deadlifting, go for it. If you don’t, skip it without guilt.

Note on Hyperextension: This is your lower back work. Don’t skip it. Most people ignore lower back and pay for it with pain later. Keep the reps controlled, don’t go heavy — feel the muscle, not the momentum.

Rest: Rest whenever you feel like you’ve had enough but watch the time. Focus on recovering, not disappearing.

On time: Keep it under 1 hour 15 minutes. If you’re going longer, tighten your rest periods or cut a set.

Wednesday — Biceps / Triceps / Forearms

Arms day. Everyone’s favorite. Hit every angle.

Exercise Sets Reps  
Hammer Curl 3 12  
Incline Dumbbell Curl 4 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior
Cable Curl 3 12-15  
Tricep Pushdown 4 🔴 Till Failure  
Bodyweight Dips 4-5 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior
Overhead Tricep Extension 3 12  
Wrist Curls 3 15  

Rest: Rest whenever you feel like you’ve had enough but watch the time. Focus on recovering, not disappearing.

On time: Keep it under 1 hour 15 minutes. If it’s going longer, you’re likely resting too long or doing too many sets.

Friday — Legs

Most people skip leg day. Most people also wonder why they look weird. Don’t skip leg day.

Exercise Sets Reps  
Barbell Squat 5 🔴 Till Failure 🟣 Heavy
Leg Extension 4 🔴 Till Failure  
Hamstring Curl 4 🔴 Till Failure ⭐ Superior
Hamstring Stretch 3 30 sec  
Calf Raises 4 20  

Note on Hamstring Curl: Don’t do this on the regular hamstring curl machine if you can avoid it. Instead, use the leg extension machine facing the other way — one leg at a time. The muscle contraction you get is so much better. It isolates the hamstring properly and you’ll actually feel it working. Try it once and you won’t go back.

Note on Calves: Calves are heavily influenced by genetics. Some people grow them just by walking, others train them for years and see minimal change. Don’t get discouraged. Keep doing calf raises consistently, go through the full range of motion, and let time do its thing. You can still build them, it just takes longer for some people.

Rest: Legs are brutal, rest whenever you feel like you’ve had enough. But watch the time. Focus on recovering, not disappearing.

On time: Legs can run long but still try to keep it under 1 hour 15 minutes. If you’re going over, tighten your rest periods. In the beginning it’s fine, legs are exhausting and you’ll need more time to recover between sets.

Saturday — Abs

Core day. Short, focused, no fluff.

Exercise Sets Reps
Plank 3 60 sec
Cable Crunch 3 15
Hanging Leg Raise 3 15
Russian Twist 3 20

Rest: Rest whenever you feel like you’ve had enough but watch the time. Focus on recovering, not disappearing.

On time: This one should be done in 40 minutes or less.

How to Up Your Game in Muscle Building & Recovery

  • Hit your daily protein goal. Generally 2.2g per kg of bodyweight or 1g per pound. Spread your protein sources throughout the day, don’t have it all in one meal.

  • Sleep is non-negotiable. You need at least 7 hours for good muscle recovery.

  • Drink enough water.

  • Micronutrients matter. People focus too much on macros and forget about micros.

Nutrition is the key

Personal Supplement Recommendations

  • Protein Powder — Use if you are not able to hit your protein goal through diet. Note for vegetarians: plant-based protein has an incomplete amino acid profile. Complete proteins like chicken and meat have all essential amino acids. If you can’t eat those, protein powder is a good option.

  • Omega-3 — Take it daily.

  • Multivitamin — Covers your micronutrient gaps.

  • Zinc & Magnesium — Take before bed. Helps with sleep quality and recovery.

  • Creatine Monohydrate — Go research this before you use it. It works. It is not a steroid. It will help you look more full. Use the monohydrate form — it’s the most researched one out there.

What I Wish I Knew When I Started Lifting

  • Don’t do cardio before weight training. Cardio depletes your energy before you even touch the weights. Save it for after.

  • You don’t actually need to warm up with light weights. In my personal opinion, unless you have a condition where you need to warm up your joints or muscles before training, you don’t need it. Just get into it.

  • Try to maintain or go above 10,000 steps a day.

  • Training abs is just as important as training any other body part.

  • If your diet is not in order, don’t expect huge changes. Training is only one half of the equation. Diet is the other — and honestly, it might be the more important half. If you’re not eating right, you’re leaving most of your gains on the table.

My “Controversial” Takes on Gym Culture

  • The gym is not a socializing place. I have very little time for myself and I cannot afford to spend it standing around having long conversations. I go in, I train, I leave. I’ve seen it happen constantly — people just standing and talking for 20 minutes between sets, barely training at all. It’s fine to be friendly, but if you’re spending more time chatting than lifting, you’re not really training.

  • Half knowledge is dangerous. Gym culture is full of people confidently giving advice they half-heard from someone else or completely misunderstood. Bad advice spreads fast in gyms. Be careful who you take advice from. If someone can’t explain why something works, they probably don’t actually know.

  • Fitness influencers are lying to you — most of them. A huge number of young people watch fitness influencers who claim to have built their physique completely naturally, no steroids, no hormone injections. People see that and set goals based on a physique that isn’t achievable naturally. Then when they can’t reach it they get sad, frustrated, and quit. The physique you’re chasing might literally require pharmaceutical help to achieve. That’s not your failure, that’s a lie you were sold. Try to watch those influencers who are open about their steroid use. I personally watch and recommend More Plates More Dates, Daddy Noel, Renaissance Periodization, and Jeff Nippard.
